How It Works Feb 25, 2026

Your Phone Already Knows Why You Slept Badly

Your smartwatch tells you that you slept 6 hours and 42 minutes. Your sleep score is 71. Your sleep app played rain sounds for 45 minutes. You wake up tired anyway.

None of these tools told you why.

Why did you wake up at 2 AM? Why was Tuesday night worse than Monday? Why do some mornings feel fine and others feel like you barely slept at all, even when the hours are the same?

The answer is almost always in your environment. And your phone already has the sensors to figure it out.

What Your Phone Can Actually Detect

Every smartphone has a microphone. That microphone can measure ambient noise levels in decibels, detect sudden sounds, and identify patterns like snoring or coughing. It does not need to record your voice. It just needs to listen to the volume and characteristics of the sound in your room.

Your phone also has a barometer, an accelerometer, and access to local weather data. Together, these can estimate changes in your room's temperature and humidity throughout the night. Not with medical precision, but with enough accuracy to spot trends and correlations.

This is what MyDriftLAB does. You place your phone on the nightstand, hit record, and go to sleep. While you rest, MyDriftLAB monitors your sleep environment using only the sensors already in your phone.

What a Morning Report Actually Looks Like

Instead of a single number, MyDriftLAB gives you a timeline of your night. A report that says things like:

Noise event detected at 2:17 AM (68 dB, 4 minutes). Estimated room temperature dropped between 1:00 AM and 3:00 AM. Snoring detected for 14 minutes between 12:30 AM and 1:15 AM.

Now you know. The garbage truck at 2:17 AM woke you up. Your heater cycled off and the room got cold. You were snoring during the first hour, which usually means you were congested or sleeping on your back.

That is actionable information. You can get earplugs. You can adjust your thermostat schedule. You can change your sleeping position. You can run an experiment in MyDriftLAB's Labs feature and measure whether the change actually made a difference.

No Wearable Required

MyDriftLAB is built around the idea that the most important sleep data is not about your body. It is about what is happening around your body. Your sleep environment is the single biggest factor you can control, and until now, nothing has been tracking it for you.

If you have an Apple Watch, MyDriftLAB can pull in heart rate, HRV, and other body metrics through HealthKit to give you even deeper correlations. But the core experience works with just your phone. No hardware to buy. No wristband to charge. No sensors to stick to your mattress.

Just your phone, your nightstand, and finally knowing why you slept the way you did.

Privacy First

MyDriftLAB processes all audio on your device. No raw audio is ever recorded, stored, or transmitted. The app only captures numeric data: decibel levels, timestamps, event durations. Your voice, your conversations, your private moments stay private. Always.
